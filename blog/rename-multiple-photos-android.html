The Problem with Default Photo Names

Every Android phone saves photos with cryptic names like IMG_20260328_143022.jpg, PXL_20260315_091445.jpg, or DSC_0001.jpg. When you have hundreds or thousands of these files, finding a specific photo becomes impossible without opening every single one.

According to Counterpoint Research (2025), the average smartphone user takes over 2,100 photos per year. That's 2,100 files with meaningless names sitting in your gallery. If you've ever tried to share vacation photos, organize project files, or back up images to a computer — you know the pain.

Batch renaming solves this by letting you rename dozens, hundreds, or even thousands of photos at once, following a consistent pattern. Instead of IMG_20260328_143022.jpg, you get Vacation_Bali_001.jpg — instantly searchable, logically organized.

How to Rename Photos by Date Taken

One of the most requested features is renaming photos using the actual date and time the photo was taken — not the file modification date, but the embedded EXIF timestamp from your camera.

Here's how to do it in Bulk Rename:

  1. Select your photos in Bulk Rename.
  2. Choose "Timestamp" from the rename operations.
  3. Pick your format:
    • YYYY-MM-DD → 2026-03-28.jpg
    • YYYY-MM-DD_HH-MM → 2026-03-28_14-30.jpg
    • DD-MM-YYYY → 28-03-2026.jpg
    • YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS → 20260328_143022.jpg
  4. Optionally add a prefix like Vacation_ to get: Vacation_2026-03-28.jpg
  5. Preview and apply.
Multiple photos on same date? Bulk Rename automatically appends serial numbers when multiple photos share the same timestamp, preventing duplicates: 2026-03-28_001.jpg, 2026-03-28_002.jpg.

Pro Tips for Organizing Photos on Android

  1. Rename right after a trip or event — while you remember what's what. It takes 30 seconds to batch rename a folder.
  2. Use consistent naming conventions — pick one format (Event_Date_Number) and stick with it across all your folders.
  3. Start serial numbers with leading zeros — use 001, 002, 003 instead of 1, 2, 3. This ensures proper alphabetical sorting up to 999 files.
  4. Include the year in date-based names — 2026-03-28 sorts better than March 28 and is globally unambiguous.
  5. Rename before backing up to cloud — Google Photos, Dropbox, and OneDrive search by filename. Meaningful names = findable photos.
  6. Keep originals with Undo — Bulk Rename's history lets you revert any rename, so you can experiment without worry.

Frequently Asked Questions
